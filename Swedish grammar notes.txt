!! Articles
Articles are words that indicate whether a noun is specific(definite) or general(indefinite)
en and ett are the indefinite(obestämd) articles
Definite (bestämd) form  is created by adding the article as a suffix
e.g. 
en bil (a car)
bilen (the car)
ett hus (a house)
huset ( the house)

When an adjective is used before a definite noun Swedish uses a double definite structure.
An additional standalone article(den det de) is placed before the adjective while the noun keeps its suffix
e.g.
den nya bilen ( the new car)
det gamma huset ( the old house)
de stora bilarna ( the new cars) -- for plural 

!! Noun
) en and ett genders
en and ett represent the genders of Swedish noun. There are no set rules to determine when they should be used. 74% of all nouns are en nouns and 26% are ett nouns. The best way to determine is to learn important ett nouns as they are few in number. Also statistically in Swedish the words ending with de te and ag are primarily ett words and words ending with a are almost always en words
